# How To Read This Atlas

## Purpose

Explain how to read this repository without overstating what the material supports.

## Reading Posture

- Treat entries as candidate signals or correspondences, not proof.
- Prefer fewer stronger entries over many weak ones.
- Distinguish direct textual signals from tool-derived outputs.
- Downgrade sources that are mainly packaging, doctrine, or scaffolding.
- Distinguish internal/operator/tool-architecture traces from independent external validation.

## Internal Trace Note

Many early atlas entries are internal/operator/tool-architecture traces from this project ecosystem. They are not presented as independent external validation. They are included because they document my own observations of where the proposed mechanism appears to pool, enact, or become operationally visible inside the development record.

Read them as:

- documented internal traces
- operator-facing traces
- tool-architecture traces

Do not read them as:

- independent external proof
- neutral third-party validation
- evidence merely because the ecosystem is large

## Material That Belongs Here

- atlas reading rules
- anti-inflation cautions
- explanation of evidential ranking

## Material That Must Not Go Here

- source summaries
- argument for the hypothesis
- new interpretive synthesis
