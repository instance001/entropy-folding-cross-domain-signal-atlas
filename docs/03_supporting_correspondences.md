# Supporting Correspondences

## Purpose

Hold secondary correspondences that echo part of the mechanism but do not document a full enough trace to carry the atlas body.

## Material That Belongs Here

- narrower structural echoes
- correspondences with more uncertainty
- downgraded or partial matches
- links to per-entry cards in `atlas/supporting/`

## Material That Must Not Go Here

- proof framing
- motif accumulation without source discipline
- packaging or tooling treated as signal

## First-Pass Supporting Set

### 1. Semantic Signal Alphabet

- Ranking: `supporting correspondence`
- Primary sources:
  - `semantic-signal-alphabet/README.md`
  - `semantic-signal-alphabet/docs/00_premise.md`
- Why it currently fits here:
  - The repo advances a bounded account of bandwidth-constrained semantic compression and reusable semantic routing.
  - The structural rhyme with entropy-folding-adjacent language is real enough to record, especially around constrained space, routing, compression, and reuse.
  - The source itself stays comparatively disciplined and does not overclaim universality.
- Why it is not mechanism-trace tier right now:
  - The repo is not directly framed as a paper about entropy folding.
  - The resemblance is meaningful, but still more correspondence than direct extension.
- What belongs here:
  - bucket count as bandwidth
  - model-native sorting under constraint
  - frozen bucket maps as stable artifacts
  - cautious note that this is a semantic-compression framework, not independent confirmation of the hypothesis
- What must not go here:
  - treating SSA as hidden proof of entropy folding
  - importing BCI or runtime adapter speculation as if it were validated signal

## Supporting-Tier Rule

An entry belongs in supporting correspondence when:

- the structural echo is real,
- the repo says something more than background packaging,
- but the connection is still less direct or less disciplined than a mechanism trace.
